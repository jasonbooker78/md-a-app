What is a CTMS?

A Clinical Trial Management System (CTMS) is the operational backbone of a research site. It tracks every study participant, visit, document, and regulatory deadline across all active protocols — replacing spreadsheets, email chains, and paper binders with a single auditable record.

ℹ
MD Anderson context: This prototype replaces a legacy QuickBase implementation that was maintained by a single SME who has since departed. Leadership is evaluating whether to Build (custom software), Align (configure an enterprise CTMS like Veeva Vault or Medidata Rave), or Backfill (rehire the SME and extend QuickBase). This demo supports the Build scenario.
CTMS
Clinical Trial Management System

Software that centralizes the operational management of clinical trials — scheduling, participant tracking, regulatory submissions, and compliance monitoring — for a research site or sponsor.

EDC
Electronic Data Capture

Software used by CRCs to record clinical observations (labs, vitals, AEs) directly into an electronic Case Report Form (eCRF). Examples: Medidata Rave, REDCap. Distinct from a CTMS, though they exchange data.

EHR / EMR
Electronic Health Record / Medical Record

The patient's clinical health record maintained by the hospital. At MD Anderson, this is Epic. The CTMS pulls lab values, vitals, and medication lists from Epic to avoid double-entry.

In app: Visit Form shows lab & vitals imported from Epic, timestamped May 19, 2026 · 07:42 AM.
eCRF
Electronic Case Report Form

The structured form used to record all protocol-required data points for a single patient visit. Every field is audited. Corrections require a reason and audit trail.

Roles & Personas

7 roles

Clinical trials involve a tightly regulated hierarchy of roles with distinct responsibilities and system access. The prototype focuses on two primary users, but all roles interact with the CTMS data.

M
Clinical Research Coordinator
CRC  ·  Primary User in Demo

The day-to-day operator of the trial at the site. Responsible for every patient-facing activity and the paper trail behind it.

Core responsibilities

  • Schedule and conduct patient visits
  • Complete eCRFs for each visit
  • Confirm lab values imported from Epic
  • Log adverse events and classify severity
  • Obtain informed consent and re-consent after amendments
  • Acknowledge protocol amendments
  • Respond to sponsor/IRB queries

Pain points the CTMS solves

  • Re-keying Epic data into eCRFs
  • Missing amendment acknowledgment deadlines
  • Manually calculating SAE FDA deadlines
D
IRB Administrator
IRB Admin  ·  Primary User in Demo

The compliance watchdog at the site. Ensures the IRB's regulatory requirements are met by all study staff and escalates failures.

Core responsibilities

  • Monitor amendment acknowledgment compliance
  • Track SAE reporting deadlines
  • Submit annual IRB renewals and amendments
  • Ensure all CRCs have completed required training
  • Maintain the regulatory binder

Pain points the CTMS solves

  • Hunting down acknowledgment status via email
  • Manually tracking FDA reporting deadlines
  • Generating compliance reports for audits
PI
Principal Investigator
PI  ·  Future User (Screen 6)

The physician-researcher legally responsible for the conduct of the trial at the site. Delegates operational work to the CRC but retains full accountability.

Core responsibilities

  • Sign off on protocol deviations
  • Review and co-sign SAE reports
  • Approve enrollment decisions
  • Oversee CRC training and delegation
S
Sponsor / CRO Monitor
Sponsor Monitor

The pharmaceutical company (or its Contract Research Organization) representative who visits the site periodically to verify data quality and protocol compliance.

Core responsibilities

  • Source data verification (SDV) — comparing eCRF to Epic
  • Raising data queries for inconsistencies
  • Issuing protocol deviation findings
  • Reviewing the regulatory binder
R
Research Pharmacist
Pharmacy

Manages investigational drug accountability — receiving, dispensing, and documenting the chain of custody for every dose of the study drug.

Core responsibilities

  • Investigational drug dispensing records
  • Temperature and storage logs
  • Drug accountability reconciliation
B
Biostatistician / Data Manager
Data Management

Manages the flow of data from the eCRF into the sponsor's database. Writes and responds to data queries, and locks the database at study end.

Core responsibilities

  • Query management (raise, respond, resolve)
  • Database lock and clean file preparation
  • Protocol deviation tracking
P
Patient / Study Participant
Subject

The enrolled patient receiving the investigational treatment. Identified in the system by a blinded Subject ID (not name) to protect privacy.

Key facts

  • Identified by Subject ID (e.g., Subject 1042), not name
  • Must provide written informed consent before any study procedures
  • Must re-consent if the ICF is updated via protocol amendment
In app: Patient R.C. · Subject 1042 · MRN-88421 on Protocol THN-204

Clinical Trial Concepts

12 terms

The operational vocabulary used daily by research staff — from the protocol that governs every decision to the visit windows that define acceptable timing.

Protocol
Study Protocol

The master document that defines every aspect of a clinical trial: the scientific hypothesis, eligibility criteria, treatment plan, visit schedule, required assessments, and statistical analysis plan. All site activities must follow it exactly.

In app: Protocol THN-204 governs the demo scenario.
Amendment
Protocol Amendment

A formal change to an approved protocol. Requires IRB re-approval before implementation. CRCs must acknowledge they've read it; enrolled patients may require re-consent depending on the nature of the change.

In app: Amendment 3 approved May 16, 2026 — updated the ICF from v1.2 to v1.3 with revised risk language.
Visit Window
Protocol Visit Window

The allowable date range within which a scheduled visit can occur. Defined in the protocol (e.g., ±3 days). Visits outside the window are protocol deviations that must be reported.

In app: Cycle 2 Day 1 window is May 17–21, 2026 (visit date: May 19).
Cycle
Treatment Cycle

A repeating unit of the treatment schedule. Oncology trials commonly use 21- or 28-day cycles. "Cycle 2 Day 1" means the first day of the second cycle of treatment.

Screening
Screening Visit

The initial visit where eligibility is assessed. Labs, imaging, and history are collected to confirm the patient meets inclusion/exclusion criteria before enrollment.

In app: Patient J.B. (Subject 1029, ONC-118) has a Screening visit at 1:00 PM in the Work Queue.
Enrollment
Study Enrollment

The act of formally entering a patient into a trial after confirming eligibility and obtaining informed consent. Enrollment metrics are tracked by the sponsor and IRB.

ANC
Absolute Neutrophil Count

A key lab value in oncology trials. Low ANC (neutropenia) may require dose delay or reduction per protocol. Normal range: 1.8–7.5 × 10⁹/L. Often checked at every cycle.

In app: R.C.'s ANC is 2.1 × 10⁹/L (within normal range) — imported from Epic.
ALT / AST
Liver Function Tests

Alanine aminotransferase (ALT) and aspartate aminotransferase (AST) are liver enzymes monitored for hepatotoxicity — a common concern with investigational oncology drugs.

In app: ALT 28 U/L, AST 31 U/L — both within normal range.
PRN
Pro Re Nata (As Needed)

Medication dosing frequency meaning "as needed." A common medication instruction in eCRFs. Concomitant medications must be listed to assess drug interactions with the investigational product.

In app: Ondansetron 8mg PRN nausea — a concomitant medication for Subject 1042.
SDV
Source Data Verification

The process by which a sponsor monitor confirms that data entered in the eCRF matches the original source (usually the EHR). Discrepancies become data queries.

Query
Data Query

A formal question raised by the sponsor's data management team or monitor when eCRF data is missing, inconsistent, or doesn't match source. CRCs must respond within a defined timeframe.

In app: Work Queue shows 2 queries due with 3 days remaining.
Deviation
Protocol Deviation

Any unintended departure from the approved protocol. Must be documented, reported to the IRB (if significant), and assessed for impact on patient safety or data integrity. Repeated deviations trigger FDA scrutiny.

Documents & Records

6 terms
ICF
Informed Consent Form

The legal and ethical document a patient signs before joining a trial. Must describe the study purpose, procedures, risks, benefits, and alternatives in plain language. Versioned — any material change requires a new version and re-consent.

In app: Amendment 3 updated the ICF from v1.2 to v1.3. Enrolled patients must be re-consented to the new version.
Reg Binder
Regulatory Binder

The complete paper (or electronic) archive of all trial documentation: IRB approvals, amendments, staff training records, delegation logs, ICF versions, and correspondence. The first thing auditors request.

DL
Delegation Log

A record of every task the PI has delegated to study staff, along with their qualifications. Only staff on the delegation log may perform the delegated tasks — a common audit finding when outdated.

ClinicalTrials.gov
Federal Trial Registry

The FDA-mandated public registry where all trials must be registered and results reported. Late registration and late results reporting carry civil monetary penalties under FDAAA 801.

IND
Investigational New Drug Application

The FDA application that permits shipment of an investigational drug across state lines for use in a clinical trial. The sponsor holds the IND; sites operate under it.

Audit Trail
Electronic Audit Trail

The 21 CFR Part 11-required record of every create, read, update, and delete action in an electronic system — who did it, when, and what changed. Non-negotiable for FDA-regulated data.

Safety Reporting

8 terms

Safety reporting is the highest-stakes workflow in a CTMS. Missed deadlines can result in FDA Warning Letters, clinical holds, and protocol suspension. The system automates classification and deadline calculation.

⚠
Key rule: Under 21 CFR 312.32, any adverse event that is serious, unexpected, and potentially related to the investigational drug must be reported to the FDA within 7 calendar days (if life-threatening) or 15 calendar days (all other SAEs). The clock starts on the day the site becomes aware.
AE
Adverse Event

Any untoward medical occurrence in a patient participating in a clinical trial, regardless of whether it's related to the study drug. All AEs must be documented in the eCRF with grade, dates, and outcome.

SAE
Serious Adverse Event

An AE that results in death, is life-threatening, requires inpatient hospitalization, causes significant disability, or is a congenital anomaly. SAEs trigger expedited FDA reporting obligations under 21 CFR 312.32.

In app: Grade ≥3 on the AE logging screen triggers SAE classification and automatically calculates the 7-day FDA deadline.
CTCAE
Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events

The NCI's standardized grading scale for adverse events, used universally in oncology trials. Grades 1–5 correspond to mild, moderate, severe, life-threatening, and death.

In app: The AE Grade dropdown uses CTCAE language: Grade 1 Mild → Grade 5 Death. Grade ≥3 = SAE.
Grade 1
Mild Adverse Event

Asymptomatic or mild symptoms; clinical or diagnostic observations only; no intervention required. Standard eCRF documentation within 7 days of awareness.

Grade 2
Moderate Adverse Event

Minimal, local or non-invasive intervention indicated; limiting age-appropriate activities of daily life. Standard documentation required, no expedited FDA reporting.

Grade 3
Severe Adverse Event → SAE threshold

Severe or medically significant but not immediately life-threatening. Hospitalization or prolongation of hospitalization indicated. Meets SAE criteria — expedited reporting required.

Grade 4
Life-Threatening Adverse Event

Life-threatening consequences; urgent intervention indicated. FDA 7-day expedited report required for unexpected events potentially related to the study drug.

Grade 5
Fatal Adverse Event

Death related to the adverse event. Triggers immediate IRB and FDA notification. The CIOMS I form is typically used for international reporting.

Compliance & Oversight

6 terms
IRB
Institutional Review Board

The independent ethics committee required by federal law to review and approve clinical research involving human subjects. At academic medical centers like MD Anderson, the IRB is an internal committee. Reviews ongoing studies annually.

In app: IRB Admin David K. monitors compliance across all active protocols from the Compliance Dashboard.
Acknowledgment
Amendment Acknowledgment

The digital signature confirming a CRC has read and understood a protocol amendment. The IRB tracks this per staff member — unacknowledged amendments are a compliance finding.

In app: Compliance Dashboard shows Maria R. and James T. acknowledged; Sandra L. is pending with a reminder due.
GCP
Good Clinical Practice

The international ethical and scientific quality standard for designing, conducting, recording, and reporting clinical trials involving humans. Codified in ICH E6 guidelines. Compliance with GCP is required for FDA-regulated trials.

FDA Audit
Bioresearch Monitoring (BIMO) Inspection

An FDA inspection of a clinical trial site reviewing data integrity, protocol compliance, and informed consent practices. Investigators are selected based on SAE reports, enrollment rates, or random selection.

Annual Review
IRB Continuing Review / Annual Renewal

The annual submission required to keep an approved protocol active. Includes an enrollment update, adverse event summary, and protocol changes. Missed renewals result in a study suspension.

Sponsor Audit
Sponsor Quality Assurance Audit

A deeper review by the pharmaceutical sponsor's QA team (distinct from monitoring visits) to confirm GCP compliance before regulatory submission. Often precedes an NDA/BLA filing.

Regulatory Framework

8 citations

The laws and regulations that the CTMS must help sites comply with. Each shapes system requirements.

Regulation What it governs CTMS implication
21 CFR Part 312 IND regulations — rules for investigational drug trials. Part 312.32 specifically governs IND safety reporting (SAE obligations). Drives the 7-day and 15-day SAE reporting deadlines and the "serious/unexpected/related" classification logic.
21 CFR Part 11 Electronic records and electronic signatures. Defines when an e-signature is legally equivalent to a wet signature. Every acknowledgment, form submission, and audit trail entry must meet Part 11 requirements.
21 CFR Part 50 Protection of human subjects — informed consent requirements. ICF versioning, re-consent triggers, and documentation of consent must be enforced by the CTMS.
21 CFR Part 56 IRB regulations — composition, operations, and review procedures. Amendment approval dates, IRB correspondence, and continuing review deadlines must be tracked.
45 CFR Part 46 The "Common Rule" — federal human subjects protections for HHS-funded research. Harmonized with FDA rules in 2018. Applies to federally funded trials at MD Anderson alongside FDA regs.
HIPAA / HITECH Patient privacy and the security of protected health information (PHI). Trials require a HIPAA authorization or waiver alongside informed consent. Subject IDs (not names) must be used in eCRFs; PHI must be encrypted at rest and in transit.
ICH E6 (R2) International Council for Harmonisation Good Clinical Practice guideline — the global GCP standard accepted by FDA, EMA, and PMDA. Defines the audit trail, delegation log, and data integrity requirements the CTMS must support.
FDAAA 801 FDA Amendments Act — mandates ClinicalTrials.gov registration and results reporting within 12 months of primary completion. Regulatory Timeline screen (Screen 6) would track these submission deadlines.

Systems & Integrations

5 systems
Epic
Epic EHR (Source of Truth for Clinical Data)

MD Anderson's electronic health record. The authoritative source for lab results, vitals, and medication lists. The CTMS pulls a timestamped snapshot from Epic at visit time to pre-populate the visit form — eliminating re-entry and reducing transcription errors.

In app: Labs and vitals on the Visit Form show "Imported from Epic · May 19, 2026 · 07:42 AM."
QuickBase
Legacy CTMS (Being Replaced)

A low-code database platform previously used as MD Anderson's CTMS. Maintained by a single SME. After that person's departure, the system is unsupported — driving the Build / Align / Backfill evaluation.

Medidata Rave
Industry-Leading EDC/CTMS Platform

The dominant enterprise EDC and CTMS in industry-sponsored oncology trials. Used by most large pharmaceutical sponsors. One of the "Align" options MD Anderson is evaluating as a QuickBase replacement.

Veeva Vault
Regulatory Document Management

A cloud-based platform for managing regulatory documents, eTMF (Trial Master File), and clinical operations. Commonly used for amendment tracking and IRB submission management.

REDCap
Research Electronic Data Capture

An academic EDC platform developed at Vanderbilt, widely used at NCI-designated cancer centers for investigator-initiated trials. Free to academic institutions. An alternative to Medidata for smaller studies.
